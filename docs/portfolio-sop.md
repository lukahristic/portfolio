# Portfolio SOP Library

> Reusable workflows extracted from this project. Each SOP should be applicable to FUTURE projects, not just this one.

---

## SOP-01 — Portfolio Discovery Interview

**Purpose:** Extract a defensible brand foundation from a person BEFORE any design/build, so the portfolio is grounded in real substance, not adjectives.

**When to use:** Start of any personal-brand or portfolio project.

**Procedure:**
1. Run discovery across 8 categories, ONE at a time, never jumping ahead:
   1. Identity & Origin
   2. What you build
   3. Strengths
   4. Goals
   5. Projects
   6. Design preferences
   7. Career direction
   8. Audience
2. **Ban adjectives.** Reject "clean," "modern," "passionate," "detail-oriented." Demand specifics, examples, and the things normally left out for being too niche/personal — the brand usually lives there.
3. **Challenge every "the audience would want this" claim.** Separate borrowed desire (what they assume they should want) from genuine desire (gut instinct). Test for internal contradiction between stated wants and actual past decisions.
4. **Surface undersold strengths.** People undervalue their real differentiators and overvalue conventional signals. Re-frame accomplishments they dismiss.
5. Do not design, wireframe, or propose sitemap until discovery yields enough for strategic recommendations.

**Key reusable principle:** *Where the stated desire and the demonstrated decisions conflict, the demonstrated decisions reveal the true taste.*

## SOP-02 — Live Decision Capture (Documentation-First)

**Purpose:** Prevent decisions from evaporating in chat; build memory/log/SOP continuously rather than reconstructing later.

**When to use:** Continuously, throughout any project.

**Procedure (on every significant decision):**
1. Update `project-memory.md` (compressed, high-density — summarize, don't dump).
2. Append the decision to `decision-log.md` (include the REJECTED alternative and WHY).
3. If a repeatable workflow emerged, create/update an SOP here.
4. Do this WITHOUT being asked.

**Compression rule:** Prefer compressed summaries over raw transcript. Maximize knowledge retained per token. Carry an "Open Threads" list so nothing is lost between sessions.

## SOP-03 — Launch-as-Narrative (turn shipping into portfolio material)

**Purpose:** Convert the act of launching a product into living portfolio content, instead of treating launch and portfolio as separate serial phases.

**When to use:** Any time a buildable product can be published to real users in parallel with portfolio work.

**Procedure:**
1. Run product launch and portfolio build in PARALLEL, never serial ("finish, then document" loses the story and invites indefinite delay).
2. Talk to real users/customers BEFORE full polish — validation, a ready user base at launch, and a "talks to customers" story (rare and high-value to founders/hiring managers).
3. Document the journey in real time: customer conversations, publishing steps, first-user reactions, what broke. This raw material becomes decision-story case studies.
4. Sequence distribution by cost/speed-to-live (e.g. cheaper/faster channel first; slower/pricier channel later) so nothing blocks going live.
5. Create SOPs DURING the work, not after (avoids the "if only I'd documented while building" regret).

**Key reusable principle:** *Traction outranks polish. "Built < shipped < real users." Get to the highest rung you can, then tell that story.*

## SOP-04 — The "Motion vs Goal" Guardrail (anti-busywork check)

**Purpose:** Catch activity that FEELS like progress but doesn't move toward the actual goal.

**When to use:** Whenever tempted to start new work, add a feature, or "keep the tools busy."

**Procedure:**
1. Name the actual goal in one sentence (e.g. "land the job," not "use the plan").
2. Ask: does this action move that goal, or does it just produce a feeling of productivity?
3. Watch for the disguised pattern — the same avoidance instinct reappears in new costumes (unpublished-but-finished work; a shiny new project; over-polishing).
4. If the urge is real but goal-irrelevant (e.g. skill curiosity), route it to a designated low-stakes sandbox ("lab") instead of letting it displace goal-work.

**Key reusable principle:** *Motion is not progress. Before starting anything new, prove it moves the one real goal — or send it to the lab.*

## SOP-05 — Question-Ordered Narrative Architecture

**Purpose:** Sequence a portfolio (or any persuasion-driven site) so it answers the viewer's evolving silent question in the order that converts, instead of defaulting to a generic section order.

**When to use:** Before wireframing any portfolio, landing page, or pitch site.

**Procedure:**
1. Map the viewer's silent question as it evolves through the scroll (e.g. "who is this? → can they do anything? → how do they think? → is this real? → what now?").
2. Assign each section exactly ONE question to answer. Cut anything that answers none.
3. Order by dependency: evidence before narrative (skeptics won't invest in a story before proof); narrative before method (method only convinces after its output is seen); relaxed/experimental content last (must not undercut serious work above).
4. Identify the ONE section that is the strongest differentiator and give it the most real estate (here: the product-thinking case study).
5. Design sections that will GROW (e.g. an in-progress launch) to accommodate evolution from day one, so progress becomes visible proof rather than a later rebuild.

**Key reusable principle:** *Order is load-bearing. Answer the right question at the right moment; lead with the differentiator, not the convention.*
