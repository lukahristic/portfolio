# Decision Log — Portfolio

> Append-only record. Decisions, rejected ideas, tradeoffs, lessons. Newest at bottom.
> Format: ID | Date/Phase | Decision | Why | Rejected alternative | Status

### D-001 | Discovery | Brand = "product builder," not "developer who uses AI"
- **Decision:** Position the person as a product builder who finds problems, designs solutions, plans, and ships — treating code as a means to an end.
- **Why:** He cannot walk through every line of code but DOES understand flow, architecture, and stack choices. Competing as a line-by-line coder loses to CS grads; competing as a product thinker plays to a real, demonstrated strength.
- **Rejected:** Hiding the AI-assisted workflow and presenting as a traditional developer. Rejected because it produces "worst of both worlds" — looks like a weaker traditional dev AND hides the actual edge.
- **Status:** Locked.

### D-002 | Discovery | Make process visible; don't rely on hiring-manager generosity
- **Decision:** Portfolio must foreground PROCESS (problem found, decisions, tradeoffs, planning, docs read, what broke & recovery) to shift the interview from "can you code?" to "how do you think?"
- **Why:** "Hope the hiring manager is generous" is not a strategy. Visible process reframes the entire evaluation onto his strongest ground.
- **Tradeoff:** Requires more writing/case-study work than a screenshot gallery.
- **Status:** Locked.

### D-003 | Discovery | Two projects play two distinct narrative roles
- **Decision:** Spottr = proof of PRODUCT THINKING. Ecommerce app = proof of TECHNICAL STRUCTURE / shipping credibility.
- **Why:** Together they cover both halves of the "product builder who can also handle real architecture" claim. The ecommerce app (auth/authz, Docker Postgres, Vercel deploy) is a genuine gatekeeper-credibility signal he was undervaluing.
- **Lesson captured:** He systematically UNDERSELLS technical work as "vibe coded." Counter this throughout.
- **Status:** Locked.

### D-004 | Discovery | Portfolio aesthetic = warm, confident, human, self-aware
- **Decision:** Visual spine is warm/approachable/self-aware with restraint. "Restraint is the flex."
- **Why:** Matches his proven instinct (he rejected intimidating B&W "premium" Spottr design for warmth — design subordinated to purpose). Also strategically rare: AI-assisted flashy portfolios are becoming the NEW generic in 2026.
- **Rejected:** Heavy 3D / parallax / scroll-trigger spectacle as the brand foundation. Rejected because (a) it's becoming generic, (b) it CAMOUFLAGES his real edge (thinking) behind effects, (c) his stated reasons ("employers would love it," "curiosity") don't justify basing brand identity on it.
- **Status:** Locked.

### D-005 | Discovery | Motion principle + experiments quarantine
- **Decision:** "Motion serves the story; the story never serves the motion." Purposeful motion only. Flashy/experimental motion is permitted but quarantined in a clearly-labeled "experiments/lab" space, framed as learning, not identity.
- **Why:** Preserves and feeds his genuine curiosity about animation technique WITHOUT letting it hijack brand identity. Separates "learn a technique" from "center brand on a technique."
- **Status:** Locked.

### D-006 | Strategy | Positioning = "product builder who ships, fast & deliberate, AI as a judgment-driven tool"
- **Decision:** Occupy the empty middle between (a) traditional junior devs who can't ship products and (b) unaware vibe-coders who can't explain decisions. Compete on "can I turn a problem into a shipped product?" not "can I code?"
- **Why:** He loses the coding-fundamentals contest to CS grads; he wins the product-judgment contest against both other camps. The middle is uncrowded because it requires judgment, which is rare.
- **Status:** Locked.

### D-007 | Strategy | Audience = design for the "believer," disarm the reasonable gatekeeper, ignore the unconvertible. Drop peers entirely.
- **Decision:** Primary target = founder/hiring-manager/small-team-lead who values shipping over pedigree. Peers are NOT an audience and converting them is NOT a goal.
- **Why:** Peers are a mirror of his insecurity, not buyers. Believer-companies treat his AI-native workflow as a feature.
- **Rejected:** Optimizing portfolio to convince skeptical peers or hard-line fundamentals gatekeepers. Rejected as misallocation.
- **Status:** Locked.

### D-008 | Strategy | Narrative spine = "I don't wait for permission to build" — projects told as DECISION stories, AI-use stated openly
- **Decision:** Portfolio is a story, not a gallery. Spottr = product-thinking chapter; ecommerce = technical-credibility chapter. Each told as decisions (saw / chose / rejected / broke / recovered). Deliberate AI workflow (plan mode, docs-first, prompt engineering) is stated as an asset.
- **Status:** Locked.

### D-009 | Strategy | Parallax/immersive interest RESOLVED: narrative immersion YES, decorative spectacle NO
- **Decision:** Validate the storytelling/immersion IMPULSE (it serves the narrative goal). Reject parallax-as-spectacle as brand vehicle. Test for every animation: "does this make my thinking clearer, or just look cool?" Clearer stays; cool-only → experiments lab.
- **Why (goal-based):** Spectacle increases friction, pulls attention from reasoning (opposite of "show how I think"), and signals generic-2026-template (collides with the judgment differentiator). Loses on his own goal scoreboard. Narrative immersion (e.g. Spottr case study that puts viewer INSIDE the intimidation problem then unfolds the solution) delivers the genuine impulse without the cost.
- **Rejected:** Heavy parallax/3D as the portfolio's organizing principle (reaffirms D-004/D-005).
- **Status:** Locked.

### D-010 | Strategy | Acknowledged risk in own recommendation
- **Decision:** Warm/restrained visual direction carries a risk of reading "not technical enough" to hard-skills screeners. Mitigate by separating TONE (warm) from CONTENT (rigorous). Visual restraint ≠ competence restraint.
- **Status:** Logged as active risk to monitor.

### D-018 | Creative Direction | Palette = own warm palette, distinct-but-related to Spottr ("house vs rooms")
- **Decision:** Portfolio gets its OWN warm palette (warm paper base / warm near-black ink / one terracotta-amber-family signature accent), same temperature family as Spottr but distinct. Each project keeps its own accent as a "room"; portfolio is the warm "house."
- **Why:** Prevents one product from hijacking the personal brand; scales as projects are added; keeps warmth (brand-critical) without subordinating identity to Spottr.
- **Rejected:** Reusing Spottr's exact palette (subordinates him to one project); fully cold/distinct palette (loses brand-critical warmth).
- **Status:** Direction locked; swatch sign-off pending.

### D-021 | Creative Direction | Signature device = transparent "built with AI, here's how"
- **Decision:** The portfolio openly demonstrates it was built with AI tools, deliberately. Medium = message; site is a live demo of the thesis.
- **Why:** Hiding it betrays the brand; showing it is the brand and is uncopyable without ringing false.
- **Status:** Locked.

### D-022 | Stack | Portfolio built in ASTRO (+ Tailwind, deploy on Vercel)
- **Decision:** Astro for the portfolio site. Tailwind CSS for styling (centralizes warm design tokens — palette + type scale). Vercel for hosting (already known from ecommerce app; native Astro support).
- **Why:** Portfolio is CONTENT, not an application. Astro ships zero-JS by default → top Core Web Vitals without manual optimization (matters for remote-intl, SEO, varied devices = locked performance budget). Islands architecture lets interactive motion experiments live as isolated islands without bloating the static story site (supports lab/house separation, D-005). Architecturally-enforced zero-JS protects the performance budget from AI-assisted bloat.
- **Brand bonus:** Spottr (React Native) + ecommerce (Next.js) + portfolio (Astro) = range across 3 tools, reinforcing "product builder who picks the right tool" over "knows one stack." "Learned a new framework for the right reason" is itself on-brand.
- **Rejected:** Next.js (to consolidate existing skill). Rejected because: ships a React runtime on every page for interactivity the site mostly lacks; less performant for static content; misses the range signal. Would be legitimate if priority were depth-over-range — not the case here.
- **Research basis:** Verified against multiple current (2026) sources; consensus strongly favors Astro for content/portfolio sites. (Documentation-first, per project rules.)
- **Status:** Locked. Resolves the stack open thread.

### D-023 | Architecture | Narrative sequence ordered by the visitor's evolving question
- **Decision:** 7 sections, each answering one stage of "who is this? → can they do anything? → how do they think? → is this real? → what now?": (1) plain confident opening, (2) proof-of-substance glance, (3) Spottr case study [product-thinking heart, narrative immersion, grows with launch], (4) ecommerce case study [technical credibility, disarms gatekeeper], (5) how-I-work + signature "built with AI" device, (6) experiments lab [looser, learning-not-identity], (7) warm low-friction close.
- **Why:** Order is load-bearing — substance before stories (skeptics need evidence first), stories before process (process only convincing after output is seen), lab near end (can't undercut serious work). Inverts the typical dev-portfolio order, which skips "how do they think" — our edge.
- **Why (growth):** Spottr section intentionally unfinished at first; visible "launching → published → real gyms" progression is itself proof of follow-through (SOP-03). Architecture accommodates this from day one.
- **Status:** Locked. Detail in content-architecture.md.

## Lessons / Patterns (reusable)
- L-01: This person's instincts are good but he defers to "what I assume the audience wants" (B&W premium app; flashy portfolio). Both times that instinct was wrong and his gut was right. **Coach toward trusting the gut; challenge "they'd want this" reasoning.**
- L-02: He undersells technical accomplishments. Actively re-frame and surface them.
- L-03: When a stated INTEREST conflicts with GOALS, separate the impulse from the vehicle. The impulse is often right (storytelling/immersion); the vehicle is often the trap (decorative parallax). Validate the impulse, redirect the vehicle. Don't just say no.
- L-04: Reusable test for any aesthetic/motion choice: "Does this make the thinking clearer, or just look cool?" Clearer stays; cool-only is quarantined.
