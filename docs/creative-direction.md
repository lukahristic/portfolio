# Creative Direction — Portfolio

> Standing reference for the build. Defines the system, not the screens.
> Read alongside project-memory.md. Status: direction proposed; palette/type personality pending user sign-off.

## Core feeling
"A thoughtful person explaining how they think — warm, unhurried, confident enough not to perform."
- Same brief he wrote for Spottr (rejected intimidating B&W premium → warm/approachable). The portfolio's enemy is the generic dev-portfolio intimidation (dark mode, neon, monospace, aggressive 3D).
- First 5 seconds should feel: "a real person who's going to tell me something" — NOT "look how many effects I can trigger."
- **Warmth is the strategy, not decoration.**

## Voice (highest-priority asset — remote-first makes writing decisive)
- Honest, plain, first-person, specific, unbothered by "supposed to." Coffee-conversation, not résumé performance.
- Self-awareness as weapon: can say what hiding-candidates can't, and it reads as confidence.
  - Signature line example: *"I didn't write every line of this app. I directed every decision in it. Here's the difference, and why it matters."*
- Say the quiet part, plainly, own it. Preempts the skeptic's objection.
- AVOID: buzzwords (passionate/results-driven/leveraging), false modesty ("just a beginner but…"), false grandeur ("full-stack ninja"). No costumes.

## Color (resolves prior open thread)
- **Decision: portfolio gets its OWN warm palette — same temperature family as Spottr, distinct identity. NOT Spottr's exact colors.**
- Mental model: **portfolio = the house (warm throughout = personal brand); each project = a room with its own accent.** Keeps brand consistent as projects are added; no single product hijacks identity.
- Proposed direction (pending swatch sign-off): warm PAPER base (soft off-white, not stark white, not dark-mode black); warm near-black INK for text (print feel, not terminal); ONE signature warm accent in the clay/terracotta/amber family (grounded, confident, deliberately NOT the default neon blue/purple). Spottr's own accent lives inside its case study.

## Typography (the quiet rebellion)
- **Direction: warm characterful SERIF for headlines/storytelling + clean humanist SANS for body/UI.** (Pending personality sign-off.)
- Why: dev portfolios are near-universally all-sans + monospace. A serif headline signals editorial / human / point-of-view = the brand. Reads as "publication," not "template."
- Contrast tells the story: serif = human thinking; sans = technical substance. Echoes "product thinker who also handles real structure."
- Humanist (warm) sans, not cold-geometric. High readability is non-negotiable for a writing-heavy remote portfolio.

## Art direction
- **Principle: show artifacts, don't claim qualities.** Real plan-mode sessions, engineered prompts, docs read, the "told the AI its design was intimidating" moment. Unfakeable proof > adjectives.
- Generous whitespace (restraint = confidence; crowding = anxiety). One idea per view.
- Experiments LAB gets a visibly looser/playful treatment — visual language itself separates "learning here" from "this is who I am."

## Motion (concrete application of D-009)
- Narrative immersion YES, decorative spectacle NO.
- Spottr case study = where motion earns its place: open INSIDE the problem (felt gym intimidation), unfold the solution on scroll.
- Test for every animation: "clearer, or just cool?" Cool-only → lab.
- Craft signals doubling as substance: respect reduced-motion; hold a performance budget (remote-intl = varied devices/connections; fast warm > heavy beautiful).

## Signature idea
- **Be transparent the portfolio itself was built with AI tools, deliberately — "here's how."** Medium becomes message; the site is a live demo of the thesis. Hiding it would betray the brand; showing it IS the brand and can't be copied without ringing false.
